Restrictions on Holding Multiple Offices

  1. This by-law gives effect to clause 8.1(e) of the Constitution.

  2. To avoid doubt, this by-law places no restriction on any member seeking to hold, or holding, any public officean elected office in a council or parliament, but places restrictions on seeking to hold, or holding offices within the Partythe incorporated association referred to in clause 40(1) in circumstances described below.

  3. While they hold a position or office listed in Column 1, no Partythe incorporated association referred to in clause 40(1) Member may hold a position or office listed in Column 2; either

    1. for more than 12 weeks; or

    2. in any circumstances described in Column 3;

    except as described in Column 4.

Convenor, Secretary, Assistant Secretary or Treasurer.

Convenor of Campaign or Policy Committee, National Councillors.

  1. In the previous clause, ‘staff’ is defined to mean those employed as staff, other than those employed on a short term temporary basis.

  2. Where another bylaw limits the holding of particular offices further than the provisions of this bylaw, the more limiting provisions will prevail.

  3. The actthe Associations Incorporation Reform Act 2012 of nominating for the positions of State Convenor, State Secretary or Convenor of the Campaign Committee carries an implicit undertaking that the person so nominating shall not, during the following 12 months, nominate for pre-selection for:

    1. The lead position on the Senate ticket, or the second position in respect of a double dissolution; or

    2. The lead position on the ticket in a Legislative Council Region, or the second position in circumstances where the Partythe incorporated association referred to in clause 40(1)’s vote in that Region in the previous election exceeded 20%; or

    3. Any state or federal seat in which the Partythe incorporated association referred to in clause 40(1)’s vote was more than 20% (or double the average vote for seats in Victoria in the most recent relevant general election, whichever is the lesser)

Should a State Convenor, State Secretary or Convenor of the Campaign Committee nominate for preselection for endorsement for any of (a) to (c) above, they shall thereby cease to hold that office.

  1. For the purpose of this bylaw, the holding of an office within the Partythe incorporated association referred to in clause 40(1) includes any holding of office on an acting basis.
