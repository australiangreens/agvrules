Chapter 1 — Objectives and Operation

  1. The Australian Greens Victoria​

    1. The body known as THE AUSTRALIAN GREENS – Victoria is:

      1. the Victorian member body of the confederation of state-based political parties known as the ‘Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626’;

      2. registered with the Australian Electoral Commission (the AECAustralian Electoral Commission) under the name of ‘Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626’ and registered with the Victorian Electoral Commission (the VECVictorian Election Commission) under the name of ‘The Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626 – Victoria’;

      3. registered with the Registrar of Incorporated Associations under the name of ‘The Greens Victoria Inc.’; and

      4. abbreviated to ‘the AGVAustralian Greens Victoria’ or ‘the Partythe incorporated association referred to in clause 40(1)’ for the purposes of this Constitution.

    2. This Constitution identifies the principles and rules governing the conduct of the AGVAustralian Greens Victoria affairs and defines the powers of the constituent groups and the roles of the positions defined within.

    3. All other AGVAustralian Greens Victoria documents are subordinate to this Constitution with the exception of the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution.

    4. The Constitution seeks to maximise opportunities for productive cooperation between AGVAustralian Greens Victoria Members and to provide protection for individual AGVAustralian Greens Victoria Members and constituent groups.

    5. The Constitution requires the constituent groups of the AGVAustralian Greens Victoria to:

      1. agree to the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution of the Greens, and to abide by this Constitution as well as to the National Constitutionthe Constitution of the Australian Greens of the Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626, and to the proper decisions of the National Organisation; and

      2. support affirmative action and practise participatory, democratic and accountable internal decision-making processes.

    6. The Constitution further requires that the AGVAustralian Greens Victoria and all its constituent groups have autonomy to make decisions relating to their own affairs, provided that these decisions remain compatible with the Constitution, the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution and the agreed policya statement of principle and intent about what government should do frameworks of the Greens.

  2. The CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution of the Greens​

    1. The CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution is the basic document of agreement which defines the principles and aims of the Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626. As such, AGVAustralian Greens Victoria Members and constituent groups should not publicly forward ideas at variance with the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution without calling into question their continued membership of, and status within, the AGVAustralian Greens Victoria.

    2. To change the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution, an amendment must be supported in a plebiscite by at least75. per cent of eligible members of the Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626 nationwide. For the result of such a plebiscite to be valid, at least51. per cent of members of the Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626 must have participated.

  3. Our Objectives​

    1. The primary purposes of the Greens Party are:

      1. to further a vision of the world characterised by peace, social justice, grassroots participatory democracy and ecological sustainability;

      2. to make, publicise and strive to implement policies on national and global issues as described in the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution;

      3. to engage in education of both ourselves and the rest of the community, to raise awareness of the issues and concerns covered in the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution;

      4. to promote and assist other individuals, associations, organisations and community networks, working for the objectives of the Greens;

      5. to promote the development of and participate and communicate with the Green political movement both nationally and globally;

      6. to be a registered political partya political party registered under Commonwealth, state or territory law pursuant to the Commonwealth and Victorian Electoral Acts;

      7. to seek the election or appointment to public officean elected office in a council or parliament of people who are committed to the CharterThe Charter of The Greens which is appended to the National Constitution, principles, policies and objectives of the Greens;

      8. to encourage other political parties to adopt our policies and principles by electoral strategies and direction of voting preferences;

      9. to coordinate mutual support and joint actions on behalf of Greens Party Members;

      10. to carry out such tasks as the membership may from time to time delegate to the State body.

  4. National and State Greens Parties​

    1. The Greens is a national confederation of State and territory parties. To facilitate smooth operations between the State and National Organisation, the State Constitution must remain broadly consistent and compatible with the National Constitutionthe Constitution of the Australian Greens.

    2. The Victorian National Delegates’ Convenor is responsible for co-ordinating the work of Victorian delegates to National Council and National Conference of the Australian Greensthe Australian Greens (The Greens) Incorporated, incorporated under the *Associations Incorporation Act 1991* (ACT) with association number A02626. The Victorian National Delegates’ Convenor must be and remain one of the AGVAustralian Greens Victoria delegates to both the National Council and the National Conference. State Council will enact a bylaw prescribing how this position is filled.

  5. Affirmative Action​

    1. The AGVAustralian Greens Victoria is committed to equal opportunity principles and the principle of women, men and gender diverse people having equal status within society.

    2. The AGVAustralian Greens Victoria will initiate affirmative action, where appropriate, for underrepresented groups, in particular: 1. First Peoples; 2. women; and 3. gender-diverse people.

    3. At no time will any AGVAustralian Greens Victoria Member be discriminated against due to gender, age, race, ethnicity, class, religion, disability, sexuality, marital status, health status, or any other perceived or actual personal attribute.
